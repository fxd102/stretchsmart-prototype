We found 6 gaps in your schedule today. Your first nudge will arrive at the next one.

What we can see: Event times, free/busy status
What we can't see: Event titles, attendees, details
What we never do: Add, edit, or delete events

How gap-finding works

1. We scan for free blocks of 3+ minutes
2. We filter to your preferred hours (default: 8am-8pm)
3. We spread nudges across your day, not back-to-back
4. If your schedule changes, nudge times adjust automatically

DESIGN DECISIONS

Q12: Google + Apple + Outlook. Read-only. These three cover 95%+ of users.

Apple limitation: CalDAV works in browser, but EventKit (better) is native-app-only. Noted in the UI so users know.

Privacy-first: We only need free/busy — not event titles or attendees. Requesting minimal permissions builds trust.

Skip is always OK: Calendar is optional. Without it, users pick their preferred nudge times manually. No feature is locked behind calendar access.

Open: Should we show event titles if we have access? Or intentionally NOT request that permission to reinforce privacy?
