V2 PROTOTYPE

Welcome Back Flows

Every return is a win. Never create guilt — guilt makes people quit permanently.

SHORT GAP (1-3 DAYS)
👋

Your shoulders could use some love. 3 minutes?

— serves the next nudge like nothing happened —

Design: Don't acknowledge the gap at all. The Anti-Metric didn't go down — it just didn't go up. No judgment in the data.

MEDIUM GAP (4-14 DAYS)
🌟

Good to see you.

Let's pick up with wall angels — an easy one to get back in the groove.

Every time you come back, your body remembers faster than you'd think. Muscle memory isn't a metaphor — it's stored in your cell nuclei and reactivates within days.

Gundersen, 2016 — myonuclei retention in skeletal muscle

Design: Warm, no gap length mentioned. Slightly easier exercise for day 1 back. One insight about why returning matters. Never shows "It's been 12 days."

LONG GAP (14+ DAYS)
🏠

Welcome home.

Your body kept what you built. Want to pick up where you were, or start fresh?

Days you've moved

47

Still here. Still yours.

Design: "Welcome home" not "Welcome back." User chooses: continuity or clean slate. Anti-Metric prominently displayed — it didn't reset. Gap length NEVER shown. Every return celebrated, never penalized.

How Others Handle This

Reframe (our model): "Welcome back! Picking up where you left off." Zero mention of gap, no streak drama. Treats every return as a win.

Noom: "Let's reset your program." Acknowledges gap but down-levels your color after inactivity. Feels like punishment.

Duolingo: "You've lost your streak!" The cautionary tale. Devastating for some users. Exactly what we don't do.

V2 RETURN FLOW DECISIONS

Q15: Three tiers: short (ignore it), medium (warm welcome), long (offer restart). Gap length NEVER displayed.

Q1: Apple-style streaks — available in stats if you look, never guilt-tripping. Return flows don't mention streaks.

Q26: Anti-Metric is the anchor. "47 — still here, still yours." Psychologically different from a reset streak.

Research: "What-the-hell effect" (Polivy & Herman) — guilt after breaking a pattern causes total abandonment. Our design deliberately avoids triggering this.
