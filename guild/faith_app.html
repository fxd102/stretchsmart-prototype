## Concept

A smart stretch/exercise reminder app that reads your calendar, finds gaps, and texts you targeted exercises.

## Requirements (confirmed by Faith)

- **Calendar:** Google Calendar or Apple Calendar integration
- **Gap detection:** Any 15+ minute gap between events is eligible
- **Frequency:** Max 4 reminders per day, at least 2 hours apart
- **Notification:** SMS text message (not email - too noisy, not push - would need native app)
- **Exercises:** Targeted to Faith's specific musculoskeletal needs
  - Phase 1: Faith provides her exercise list manually
  - Phase 2: App takes a list of issues (e.g. "tight hip flexors", "desk neck") and suggests exercises
- **Exercise presentation:** TBD - Faith is thinking about how she wants these displayed

## Architecture (draft)

```
[Google/Apple Calendar API] → [Backend server] → [SMS via Twilio/similar]
                                    ↓
                              [Exercise DB]
                              [Scheduling logic]
```

### Components
1. **Calendar reader** - OAuth to Google Calendar API (or Apple CalDAV)
2. **Gap finder** - Scans today's events, finds 15+ min gaps
3. **Scheduler** - Picks up to 4 windows, 2+ hours apart, from available gaps
4. **Exercise selector** - Cycles through exercises targeted to user's needs
5. **SMS sender** - Sends text with exercise name + details
6. **Web dashboard** (optional) - Configure exercises, view history, manage settings

## Product Vision (evolved Mar 7)

### Onboarding
- Noom-style quiz: "Pick what matters to you" (3 of ~6 options: back pain, posture, headaches, independence, sleep, stiffness)
- No lengthy intake — 2 minutes max

### Daily Flow (revised)
- Each day has ONE focus exercise (not random each nudge)
- Every nudge that day = same exercise, reinforcing the habit
- Rotation: cycle through ~15 exercises over 2-3 months
- Once an exercise is "habitualized," introduce the next one
- Variety comes from daily rotation, not per-nudge randomization
- Under 2 minutes per nudge

### The Path (journey metaphor)
- Visual trail map based on user's picks
- Each completed exercise = forward progress
- Milestones every ~5 stops unlock bite-sized insights (2-3 sentences, not essays)
- Streak tracking built into the trail

### Content Philosophy
- Insights must be SHORT (2-3 sentences max for general audience)
- Full causal chain: action → mechanism → real-world outcome
- Peter Attia longevity flavor
- Detailed insights saved in faith_app_insights.json (20 drafted, need shortening)

## Design Philosophy (Faith's vision, Mar 7)

- **Surprise/variety is key** - user shouldn't know what exercise is coming. Not "same old same old."
- **Behavior change app**, not PT homework. Think Reframe (alcohol app) psychology.
- **Engagement tactics** (inspired by Reframe): gamification, streaks, randomized selection, encouraging messages, bite-sized content
- **Light and motivating** - not clunky or effortful like Hinge Health / MedBridge GO

## Competitive Research (Mar 7)

- **Hinge Health**: Employer-provided virtual PT, 10-15 min video sessions, camera-based. Adapts to pain. Complaints: glitches, wrong exercises that increase pain. Not consumer-facing.
- **MedBridge GO**: PT-assigned exercises, video demos, timers, gamification. Complaints: broken progress tracking, clunky UI. Tied to specific PT relationship.
- **Our differentiators**: Calendar-aware timing, light touch (text not app session), motivating not homework, PT-quality plans without appointment (via Faith's aunt's expertise)
